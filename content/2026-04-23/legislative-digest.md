## 🔴 ACT NOW

### Digital Omnibus on AI — political trilogue targeting compromise text 🆕 NEW
- **Organiser:** Cyprus Presidency of the Council of the EU, European Parliament (IMCO/LIBE co-rapporteurs), European Commission
- **Jurisdiction:** EU
- **Stage:** trilogue
- **Deadline:** 2026-04-28 (next political trilogue)
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.europarl.europa.eu/legislative-train/package-digital-package/file-digital-omnibus-on-ai
  - https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/digital-omnibus-ai-regulation-proposal
  - https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/03/13/council-agrees-position-to-streamline-rules-on-artificial-intelligence/
  - https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/03/meps-adopt-joint-position-on-proposed-digital-omnibus-on-ai/
- **Why it matters:** Trilogue entered on 26 March 2026 after Council (13 March) and Parliament (second March plenary) adopted negotiating positions. The 28 April political meeting is the first serious attempt at compromise text. Open divergences matter for frontier-risk governance: (i) whether the AI Office retains supervisory competence over AI systems built on a GPAI model where the model provider and the system deployer are the same entity — both co-legislators reinforce this against the Commission's softer draft; (ii) the fixed application date for Annex III high-risk systems (Parliament locks in 2 December 2027; Council allows flexibility tied to standards availability); (iii) the shape of a new Article 5 prohibition on non-consensual sexual deepfake generators. Any slippage of the AI Office's remit is the file SaferAI should track most closely.

- **Organiser:** Colorado General Assembly / Governor's AI Policy Work Group
- **Jurisdiction:** US (Colorado)
- **Stage:** in-force (delayed enforcement) / superseded-in-progress
- **Deadline:** 2026-06-30 (enforcement date under SB 25B-004)
- **Sources:**
  - https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb24-205
  - https://www.hunton.com/privacy-and-information-security-law/enforcement-of-colorado-ai-act-delayed-until-june-2026
  - https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/the-colorado-ai-policy-work-group-proposes-an-updated-framework-to-replace-the-colorado-ai-act
- **Why it matters:** The Polis-appointed Work Group proposed on 17 March 2026 to replace SB24-205 with an Automated Decision-Making Technology (ADMT) framework effective 1 January 2027. Whether the 30 June 2026 enforcement date still binds developers of high-risk systems depends on legislative action during Colorado's short session. The Trump Administration's December 2025 preemption EO adds further uncertainty — this is now a federal-state preemption test case, not a routine state-law compliance exercise.
- **confidence:** medium
- **why:** The replacement framework is a proposal, not enacted law; the 30 June compliance date currently stands but may be amended mid-period.

- **Jurisdiction:** UK
- **Stage:** published (policy input)
- **Deadline:** n/a (published 2026-04-10)
- **Sources:**

  - https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-security-institute-frontier-ai-trends-report-factsheet/ai-security-institute-frontier-ai-trends-report-factsheet
- **Why it matters:** AISI's first public trends report consolidates evaluation results across 30+ frontier models since late 2023, documenting a 2025 jump to expert-level cyber-task completion in a tested model. Follow-up blog posts (21–22 April) report that a sandboxed open-source agent reconstructed AISI's internal infrastructure, and that AISI's red team has "found vulnerabilities in every single system we have tested". These findings are the empirical backbone for UK government statements on statutory duties; expect DSIT to lean on this report in any response to the Lords' AI Regulation Bill.

## 💡 EDITORIAL NOTE

The Omnibus trilogue is doing two things at once: it is the simplification exercise the Commission advertised, and it is the first structural renegotiation of the AI Act's governance architecture since adoption. The AI Office's supervisory competence over GPAI-derived systems is the single provision whose erosion would matter most for SaferAI's frontier-risk agenda — both co-legislators are, for now, reinforcing it, which is a favourable political moment to lock that reinforcement into the final text. Second-order reading: with the US framework preempting state-level safety law and the UK AISI publishing empirical findings rather than draft rules, the EU is once again the only jurisdiction where binding frontier-risk obligations are actually being negotiated. That is a narrow political window — Article 92/93/101 enforcement powers activate on 2 August 2026, and the Omnibus determines how hard that lever will actually be.
