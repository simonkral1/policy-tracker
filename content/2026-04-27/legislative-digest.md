### Digital Omnibus on AI — political trilogue (Cyprus Presidency target for compromise)
- **Organiser:** Cyprus Presidency of the Council of the EU, European Parliament (IMCO/LIBE co-rapporteurs), European Commission
- **Jurisdiction:** EU
- **Stage:** trilogue
- **Deadline:** 2026-04-28 (political trilogue)
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.europarl.europa.eu/legislative-train/package-digital-package/file-digital-omnibus-on-ai
  - https://www.aoshearman.com/en/insights/digital-omnibus-on-ai-what-is-really-on-the-table-as-trilogues-begin
  - https://www.lewissilkin.com/en/insights/2026/04/01/the-latest-on-the-digital-omnibus-on-ai-102momk
  - https://www.kaizenner.eu/post/ai-act-omnibus
- **Why it matters:** Tomorrow's meeting is the Cyprus Presidency's target for political agreement, with formal adoption expected by July ahead of the 2 August enforcement powers. State-of-play: co-legislators have converged on fixed postponement dates (2 December 2027 for Annex III stand-alone high-risk; 2 August 2028 for Annex I product-embedded systems), on bans on non-consensual sexual deepfakes and CSAM generators, and on reinstating a simplified registration obligation for non-high-risk systems. Open questions: (i) whether Parliament's move of all Annex I-A categories into Annex I-B (horizontal AI-Act integration into machinery, toys, RED, MDR, PED, PPE etc.) survives — Council has not addressed this; (ii) the synthetic-content marking date (Council 2 February 2027 vs Parliament 2 November 2026); (iii) the AI Office's supervisory competence over GPAI-derived systems — the file SaferAI must track most closely. Failure to land tomorrow pushes any deal into May plenary territory and squeezes the runway before 2 August.

## 🟢 PIPELINE WATCH

### UK AISI evaluation of Claude Mythos Preview — first frontier cyber-capability finding of an unreleasable model 🆕 NEW
- **Organiser:** UK AI Security Institute (DSIT)
- **Jurisdiction:** UK
- **Stage:** published (policy input)
- **Deadline:** n/a (published 2026-04-13)
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities
  - https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/
  - https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/14/claude-mythos-test-attack-capabilities-limits/
  - https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/04/anthropic-mythos-ai-cybersecurity/
- **Why it matters:** AISI's 13 April evaluation is the first published government finding that a frontier model has crossed a capability threshold that made its developer choose not to release it. Headline numbers AISI reports: 73% success on expert-level capture-the-flag tasks (from 0% pre-April 2025); first model to autonomously complete a 32-step end-to-end network compromise (3 of 10 attempts; mean 22 of 32 steps); identified and exploited zero-days across every major OS and browser tested. Anthropic's Project Glasswing kept the model out of general release and gated to selected vendors. Two policy implications: (1) AISI's empirical evidence is the strongest single argument so far for the Article 92/101 enforcement powers SaferAI cares about — co-legislators in tomorrow's trilogue can no longer treat systemic risk as hypothetical; (2) it puts the UK's still-unintroduced AI Regulation Bill under fresh pressure, with Lord Holmes's private member's bill expected to be retabled after the next King's Speech.

### Project Glasswing unauthorised-access incident — supply-chain breach of a not-released frontier model 🆕 NEW
- **Organiser:** Anthropic (model developer); third-party contractor implicated; reported by Bloomberg
- **Jurisdiction:** US (with EU/UK supervisory implications)
- **Stage:** investigation (ongoing)
- **Deadline:** TBC — incident reported 2026-04-21 to 2026-04-22
- **Sources:**
  - https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/21/unauthorized-group-has-gained-access-to-anthropics-exclusive-cyber-tool-mythos-report-claims/
  - https://www.engadget.com/ai/anthropic-is-investigating-unauthorized-access-of-its-mythos-cybersecurity-tool-091017168.html
  - https://www.cbsnews.com/news/anthropic-investigates-mythos-ai-breach/
- **Why it matters:** Reporting indicates a small group obtained access to Mythos Preview via credentials of a member working at a third-party Anthropic contractor, combined with information from a separate breach at AI-recruitment vendor Mercor and URL-pattern guessing. No safety-layer bypass, but vendor-environment access to a model the developer assessed as too capable for general release. Three live policy questions: (i) under California SB 53's catastrophic-incident reporting duties (in force since 1 January 2026), does this qualify as a reportable critical safety incident? (ii) does the incident validate the AI Office's pursuit of supervisory competence over GPAI-derived systems and downstream deployers in the Omnibus negotiation? (iii) the Council of Europe AI Convention's CDNET committee — operational from 1 January 2026 — gains a concrete case study for systemic-risk methodology. SaferAI should brief Chloé to track Anthropic's incident report, FTC/DOJ posture under the December EO, and any CDNET reference to the case.
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Why (confidence):** Reporting based on Bloomberg sourcing; Anthropic confirms an investigation but not the full chain of access. No regulator has yet publicly opened a formal proceeding.

## 💡 EDITORIAL NOTE

The week's structural shift is that Mythos has collapsed the distance between a frontier-risk policy debate and the empirical evidence the debate has historically lacked. AISI's 13 April evaluation is the first time a national security institute has published, with numbers, that a frontier system has reached a capability that caused its developer to withhold release; the unauthorised-access reporting eight days later is the first time such a model is reported to have leaked through a third-party vendor environment despite that withholding. Both events land 24 hours before the Digital Omnibus political trilogue and 97 days before Article 92/101 enforcement powers activate — a sequencing that materially raises the political cost of weakening the AI Office's supervisory remit in tomorrow's text. Second-order: the same evidence base reframes the US federal-versus-state preemption fight, where the DOJ Litigation Task Force is now poised to challenge precisely the state laws (Colorado SB24-205, California SB 53) whose triggers Mythos most clearly satisfies. SaferAI should be ready to translate AISI's findings into the trilogue room and into a Mercor-style supply-chain commentary for CDNET and any FTC/DOJ public-comment window.
