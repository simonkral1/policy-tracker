### GPT-5.5 and the broken state of government evals 🆕 NEW
- **URL:** https://www.transformernews.ai/p/openai-shouldnt-be-deciding-if-its-gpt-55
- **Published:** 2026-04-24
- **Stage:** analysis
- **Why it matters:** Shakeel Hashim's lead from this week argues that GPT-5.5's release exposes a structural failure in pre-deployment evaluation. AISI assessed GPT-5.5 as the world's most capable model on individual cyber tasks (it can complete a 32-step corporate-network attack simulation estimated to take a human expert ~20 hours), and AISI red-teamers found a universal jailbreak with six hours of work — but AISI was *not* able to verify the effectiveness of OpenAI's final post-jailbreak configuration, and OpenAI has no obligation to wait for them. This is the strongest recent argument for putting AISI's evaluation findings on a binding rather than advisory footing, and lands directly into the Article 92/101 enforcement-design conversation that SaferAI is tracking; consider citing it in any Omnibus-window submission about the AI Office's relationship to national institutes.

- **URL:** https://www.transformernews.ai/p/pentagon-anthropic-mythos-cybersecurity-hacking-trump-hegseth
- **Published:** 2026-04-21 (approximate; piece references the 19–20 April Axios/CBS scoop on NSA Mythos use)
- **Stage:** reported
- **Why it matters:** Transformer's framing of the Hegseth–Emil Michael–Trump decision to designate Anthropic a supply-chain risk pivots on the Axios/CBS reporting that the NSA is using Mythos anyway, despite the DoD blacklist.
