## 📖 Deep dives

### AISI evaluation of Claude Mythos Preview's cyber capabilities 🆕 NEW
- **Authors:** UK AI Security Institute Cyber team
- **Venue:** UK AI Security Institute (blog + technical evaluation)
- **arXiv ID:** null
- **URL:** https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities
- **Stage:** published
- **Summary:** AISI reports that Claude Mythos Preview is the first frontier model to autonomously complete its 32-step corporate-network attack range "The Last Ones" — succeeding 3 out of 10 attempts end-to-end and averaging 22 of 32 steps; the next-best model evaluated, Claude Opus 4.6, averaged 16 steps. On expert-level capture-the-flag tasks no frontier model could complete a year ago, Mythos Preview now succeeds 73% of the time, and AISI assesses that frontier cyber-offence capability is doubling roughly every four months. The policy implication is that "expert human professional in days of work" is now within range of a single deployed model when explicitly directed and given network access — though AISI is careful that its ranges have no live defenders or endpoint detection, so the claim is bounded to weakly-defended systems. For SaferAI's policy team, the Mythos evaluation is not just another capability-uplift datapoint: it is the first concrete public evidence that a model has crossed an autonomy threshold the GPAI Code of Practice systemic-risk taxonomy treats as a Critical Capability Level, and it lands inside the AI Act's GPAI obligations window. Note: the AISI report itself was published 13 April 2026, one day before this digest's strict period; surfacing here because it dominates the fortnight's policy reading and was not covered in the previous digest.
- **Why it matters to SaferAI:** Provides the first publicly-reported pre-deployment evaluation in which a frontier model has cleared a multi-day autonomous cyber-attack threshold — concrete evidence to anchor SaferAI's positioning on cyber-CCL thresholds in the GPAI Code of Practice and on the urgency of independent-evaluator access provisions in the AI Act systemic-risk regime.
